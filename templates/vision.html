The Vision

Lately, I’ve found it harder to come across work—especially in the mainstream—that feels personal, unbound, or truly lived. And the more I analyze the type of work that inspired me to start a journey of my own, the more I realize that it is work of that nature that has helped me find meaning through art.

Maybe my own work disappears into obscurity. Maybe it’s unearthed decades after I’m gone. Maybe it resonates and takes off tomorrow; I have no idea.

What I do know is this: my intention is to try to create work that speaks to us all—drawn from a place of truth, not trend. I’m not building to hit targets. My goal is to build something that endures the test of time. A body of work that stands for something; not just made to land, but to last.

Meanwhile, my Instagram remains a place where I share snippets of both my original work and covers of songs that have inspired me along my journey.

If you’re listening/watching/reading—realize that it means more to me than you will ever know.
